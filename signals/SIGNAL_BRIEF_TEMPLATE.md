# Signal Brief Template

Use this template when a development in AI, crypto, tech, markets, or adjacent systems seems meaningful enough to preserve.

## Purpose

Turn scattered interesting developments into repeatable, ranked, reusable signal briefs.

## What counts as a signal

A signal is not just news. It is a development that may matter because it suggests:
- a change in direction
- a change in capability
- a new constraint
- a new opportunity
- a hidden shift becoming more visible
- a repeated pattern worth tracking over time

## Exclusions

Do not log something just because it is loud, dramatic, or trending.
Ignore items that are:
- pure noise
- low-substance engagement bait
- repetitive with no new implication
- too vague to interpret
- interesting but not useful

## Brief format

### Title
Short and specific.

### Date
YYYY-MM-DD

### Domain
Examples: AI / crypto / hardware / regulation / markets / agent systems / social platform

### Signal summary
2-4 sentences on what happened.

### Why it matters
State the concrete implication, not just the event.

### Signal strength
- weak
- medium
- strong
- watchlist

### Time horizon
- immediate
- near-term
- medium-term
- long-tail

### Confidence
- low
- moderate
- high

### What changed
Describe the delta versus prior state.

### What might follow
List 1-3 likely downstream effects.

### Action stance
Choose one:
- ignore
- watch
- research deeper
- integrate into workflow
- revisit later

### Notes
Anything useful that does not fit above.

## Ranking guidance

### Strong
Use when the item clearly changes your model of what matters.

### Watchlist
Use when the signal is incomplete but plausibly important.

### Medium
Use when the signal is real but its implications are still narrow.

### Weak
Use when something is mildly useful but not worth much attention.

## Anti-noise rule

A small real shift beats a large noisy headline.

## Output rule

When possible, each signal brief should help one of these:
- improve future research
- improve decision quality
- improve system direction
- improve timing or attention allocation
- produce a reusable artifact or watch item
