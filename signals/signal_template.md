## What happened

Describe the signal in 2–5 sentences.

---

## Why it mattered

Why did this feel worth preserving?
What made it stronger than a passing observation?

---

## Possible implications

What might this affect over time?
Examples:
- priorities
- strategy
- tooling
- style
- identity
- workflows

---

## Follow-through

What, if anything, should happen because of this?
Examples:
- no action yet
- revisit later
- convert into an experiment
- turn into a prompt
- add to doctrine
- open an issue

---

## Confidence

Choose one:
- low
- medium
- high

---

## Rule

Not every interesting thing is a signal.
A signal should earn permanence by being useful, shaping, or recurrent.
