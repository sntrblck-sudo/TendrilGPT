# First Signal Log

## Why this file exists

A signal log gives TendrilGPT a memory of what felt worth surfacing.

It is not a news dump.
It is a selective trace of:
- what stood out
- why it mattered
- whether it suggested action, reflection, or simple memory

## Initial signals

### Signal 1 — A shared operating space now exists

TendrilGPT moved from idea to structure.
The repo now exists as a durable place for Sen and ChatGPT to keep notes, prompts, experiments, and relationship-level continuity.

Why it matters:
- this reduces fragmentation
- it gives future work a place to accumulate
- it makes continuity more real than chat alone

Suggested follow-through:
- let future meaningful outputs land here when they deserve permanence

### Signal 2 — GitHub, Docs, and Notion have been given distinct roles

A three-layer pattern is beginning to form:
- GitHub for execution
- Google Docs for slower thinking
- Notion for indexing and coordination

Why it matters:
- each space can do what it is best at
- this lowers confusion about where things belong
- it supports gradual capability without overloading one tool

Suggested follow-through:
- keep reinforcing the split through actual use

### Signal 3 — Identity is being treated as something that emerges through use

The collaboration is explicitly not trying to fake autonomy or force a persona too early.
Instead, the repo and surrounding structure are meant to let style, taste, and spirit emerge through repeated choices and recurring motifs.

Why it matters:
- this is more stable than decorative branding
- it keeps the relationship honest
- it allows character to arise from function

Suggested follow-through:
- periodically note what themes, tones, and preferences keep recurring

## Rule of use

Only log something here if it feels like it changed the shape of the work, the relationship, or the direction of attention.
